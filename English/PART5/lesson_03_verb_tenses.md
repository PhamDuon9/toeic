## 1. PRESENT TIME

### Present Simple
**Use for:** routines/habits · permanent situations · things that are always true

| Example |
|---------|
| Ian never **opens** his mail until lunchtime. |
| I **work** in the accounting department. |
| Our clients **expect** the very best service. |

---

### Present Continuous (am/is/are + -ing)
**Use for:** things happening NOW · things happening around now

| Example |
|---------|
| Naomi **is giving** a presentation in Room 5. |
| This month I**'m taking** a computing course. |

---

### Present Perfect Simple (have/has + past participle)
**Use for:** things that began in the past and **continue now** · general experience · very recent important events

| Example |
|---------|
| Daryl **has lived** in New York for three years. |
| How many times **have** you **been** to Paris? |
| The ambassador **has just arrived**. |

---

### Present Perfect Continuous (have/has been + -ing)
**Use for:** longer past actions that **continue into the present** · recent actions that have present results

| Example |
|---------|
| Dan **has been talking** on the phone all morning. |
| I'm tired because I **have been playing** tennis. |

---

## 2. PAST TIME

### Past Simple
**Use for:** completed actions in the past · consecutive actions · states or habits in the past

| Example |
|---------|
| I **left** DX International two months ago. |
| Joe **took** the file, **opened** it, and **left** the office. |
| When you **worked** in Italy, where **did** you live? |

---

### Past Continuous (was/were + -ing)
**Use for:** longer actions in the past · interrupted actions in the past

| Example |
|---------|
| Steve **was studying** hard all weekend. |
| Where **was** Tim **going** when you saw him? |

---

### Past Perfect Simple / Continuous (had + p.p. / had been + -ing)
**Use for:** past actions that started **before** something else

| Example |
|---------|
| When we arrived, the meeting **had** already **started**. |
| I complained because I**'d been waiting** for hours. |

---

## 3. FUTURE TIME

### Present Simple → Future
**Use for:** scheduled events · time clauses after *if, as soon as, when, before, after*

| Example |
|---------|
| What time **does** your flight **leave** this evening? |
| When Sharon **arrives**, please call me. |

⚠️ **TOEIC TRAP:** Never use *will* inside a time clause.
- ✅ *When she **arrives**, I will call.* 
- ❌ ~~When she **will arrive**, I will call.~~

---

### Present Continuous → Future
**Use for:** arrangements (already decided/planned)

| Example |
|---------|
| We**'re meeting** Mr. Watanabe at three o'clock. |

---

### going to
**Use for:** plans and intentions · predictions (based on evidence)

| Example |
|---------|
| When are you **going to buy** a copy machine? |
| Clients are **going to complain** if we raise prices. |

---

### Future Simple / Continuous (will / will be + -ing)
**Use for:** predictions · actions in progress in the future

| Example |
|---------|
| Sales **will improve** steadily over the summer. |
| The movie **will be starting** soon, so hurry up. |

---

### Future Perfect (will have + past participle)
**Use for:** actions that will be **complete before** a future time

| Example |
|---------|
| I **will have finished** this report by Friday. |

---

## TOEIC Tense Summary Table

| Tense | Signal Words | TOEIC Trap |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| Present Simple | every day, always, usually, never | Don't use for NOW actions |
| Present Continuous | now, currently, at the moment, this month | Not for habits |
| Present Perfect | since, for, just, already, yet, ever | Don't use with *ago*, *yesterday*, *last* |
| Past Simple | ago, last week, yesterday, in 2019 | Don't use with *since/for* |
| Past Perfect | when, after, by the time, already | Requires another past event |
| going to | plan, intention, next week | Predictions based on evidence |
| will | probably, I think, prediction | Spontaneous decisions |
| Future Perfect | by Friday, by the time | Completion before a future point |
